## Alternating Archive

Alternating Archive treats time as a physical object: not a list, but a corridor you walk through. The primary gesture is oscillation—left, right, left, right—so that chronology becomes spatial rhythm. The work must feel meticulously crafted, the product of deep expertise, where the cadence of spacing, alignment, and scale reads like a disciplined score rather than decoration.

Materiality is expressed through restraint: paper-white fields, graphite lines, and a single warm accent that appears only when meaning demands it. Surfaces remain clean and quiet so the eye can register the smallest shift in weight or tone. Every mark is placed with painstaking attention, tuned until the composition feels inevitable—master-level execution that suggests countless revisions.

Scale carries hierarchy. Years are monumental—serif numerals with air around them—while actions and labels are whispered in tight, clinical microtype. Information is encoded in distance: the dot’s offset, the year’s lean toward or away from the centerline, the consistent margin that frames the whole piece. Nothing is “added”; everything is calibrated.

Composition follows a strict internal geometry. A central axis is implied, never over-explained: nodes orbit it in alternating polarity, creating tension and release. The path itself is absent—no dotted trails, no ornamental diagrams—so the viewer completes the line mentally. This omission is deliberate and painstaking, a confident subtraction that elevates the artifact beyond UI and into editorial object.

Craftsmanship is non-negotiable. The final form should look as though it took countless hours of refinement by someone at the absolute top of their field: every gap consistent, every edge crisp, every typographic decision considered. The result is quiet, rigorous, and expensive—an archive rendered as choreography.
